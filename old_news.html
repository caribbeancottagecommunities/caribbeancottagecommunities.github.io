TACA IS BACK! - 1 June 2009
Starting June 12th TACA airlines will be again offering service to both Roatan and San Pedro Sula. After discontinuing service after the price of oil spiked last year, the airline has decided to once again start things up. For those not familiar, TACA is a very large airline that serviced the island for years previous to either Delta and Continental. Many from the U.S. who do not travel to Central and South America may not recognize the name but TACA fly out of Los Angeles, New York, Houston, Miami and run one of the most modern fleets in the world, (normally Airbus). For those looking for a bargain ride to the island, the price offered right now is only $348.00 return from Miami with flights on Thursday and Sunday. From what we have been told this is a four month test to see if the market is viable once again for them. A great time to fly at prices not seen in many years. If you are looking for that vacation to decompress, research, or view our great summer special on the few remaining lots, now is a really great time for your pocketbook.

UPDATE FROM ROATAN ON ECONOMIC OUTLOOK - 15 Apr 2009
Roatan will soon have its first comprehensive full service hospital. This is something we have long awaited and is great news. Other infrastructure continues to be improved and recently all paved roads were repaired from rainy season damage. A new water treatment plant is being built in Flowers Bay. The second cruise ship dock (Carnival Line) is nearly complete at Dixon Cove and they have signed a 30 year lease for Coral Cay and are near completion of their resort there as well. Economic events up north have affected the island economy but Roatan is still growing and moving ahead much more quickly. Its a great time to buy on Roatan!
